## Positioning
- One-liner: High-performance JavaScript compilation and optimization tool for efficient development with Rust and SWC technology.
- Alternatives:
  1. "Turbo-charged JavaScript development with Rust-powered optimization."
  2. "Revolutionize your JavaScript workflow with SWC-enhanced compilation."
  3. "Achieve lightning-fast JavaScript performance with the power of Rust and SWC."

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
### Persona 1 - John Doe, Senior Frontend Developer, Budget: $1000/year
- Name: John Doe
- Daily Job: Leading a team of frontend developers, building complex web applications.
- Pain Point: Inefficient JavaScript compilation and optimization processes that slow down development and increase costs.

### Persona 2 - Jane Smith, Freelance Developer, Budget: $500/year
- Name: Jane Smith
- Daily Job: Building various web projects for clients, seeking tools to optimize development workflow and improve client satisfaction.
- Pain Point: Lack of efficient JavaScript compilation and optimization tools that can help deliver projects faster and at a lower cost.

### Persona 3 - Michael Brown, Startup Founder, Budget: $2000/year
- Name: Michael Brown
- Daily Job: Building and scaling a startup, looking for tools to improve development efficiency and reduce costs.
- Pain Point: Inefficient JavaScript compilation and optimization processes that hinder the startup's growth and competitiveness.

## Channels
- Channel 1 - GitHub (CAC estimate: $0)
  - Leverage GitHub's community and developer-focused audience to promote the product.
- Channel 2 - Dev.to (CAC estimate: $500)
  - Publish articles, tutorials, and case studies to reach a wider developer audience.
- Channel 3 - Twitter (CAC estimate: $250)
  - Utilize Twitter to engage with developers, share updates, and promote the product.

## Content Cadence
- Week 1: Introduction post on GitHub and Dev.to, teaser tweets.
- Week 2: In-depth tutorial on JavaScript optimization with js-optimizer, case study on a real-world project.
- Week 3: Comparison with other JavaScript optimization tools, Q&A session on Twitter.
- Week 4: Product update and feature announcement, live demo on Twitch.
- Ongoing: Regular updates, bug fixes, and improvements.

## Launch Milestones
- D-30: Finalize product features, complete content creation, and set up marketing channels.
- D-0: Launch the product on GitHub, publish content on Dev.to and Twitter.
- D+30: Evaluate user feedback, make necessary adjustments, and publish follow-up content.
- D+90: Review success metrics (DAU, $MRR target), analyze user engagement, and plan for future improvements.

## Success Metrics
- DAU (Daily Active Users): 100 by D+30, 500 by D+90.
- $MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) target by D+90: $10,000.
