## Customer Journey – **js‑optimizer**

| Phase | Trigger Event | Friction Points | User Emotions | Opportunities to Delight | Success Metric |
|-------|---------------|----------------|---------------|--------------------------|----------------|
| **Aware** | • A front‑end team reads a blog post about “Why JavaScript builds are slowing you down”.<br>• A CI/CD pipeline alert shows build times > 30 s for a medium‑size repo. | • Over‑whelming number of competing tools (Babel, Terser, esbuild, Vite).<br>• Lack of clear performance benchmarks for each tool. | Curious, slightly frustrated (builds are a bottleneck). | • Publish a concise “JS‑Optimizer vs. the rest” benchmark graphic (e.g., 2× faster than Babel, 30 % smaller bundles vs. Terser).<br>• Offer a 30‑second explainer video that shows a Rust‑powered compile in action. | **Impression rate** – % of target devs who click the benchmark graphic (goal ≥ 12 %). |
| **Consider** | • Engineer receives a recommendation from a teammate or a Slack poll asking “What tool should we try to cut build time?”. | • Uncertainty about integration effort with existing toolchains (Webpack, Vite, Next.js).<br>• Concerns about stability of a Rust‑based plugin. | Analytical, cautious optimism. | • Provide a **one‑click npm installer** (`npm i -D js-optimizer`) that auto‑detects the bundler and configures itself.<br>• Publish a “Migration Checklist” PDF with risk‑mitigation steps and a 24 h support window. | **Evaluation conversion** – % of visitors who download the checklist or start the installer (goal ≥ 8 %). |
| **Try** | • Developer runs `npx js-optimizer --dry-run` on a feature branch. | • Build failures due to edge‑case syntax or custom Babel plugins.<br>• Limited visibility into what optimizations were applied. | Hopeful but nervous (fear of breaking CI). | • Show a **real‑time optimization dashboard** (CLI table + optional web UI) that lists transformed files, size reductions, and time saved.<br>• Offer a **fallback mode** that automatically reverts to the previous build if any error occurs. | **Trial success rate** – % of trial runs that complete without fallback (goal ≥ 85 %). |
| **Adopt** | • CI pipeline passes with a 25 % reduction in build time and 15 % smaller bundles. | • Ongoing maintenance: keeping the Rust binary up‑to‑date, handling version mismatches. | Satisfied, empowered (clear ROI). | • Provide **auto‑update hooks** (`js-optimizer upgrade`) and a **semantic‑version compatibility matrix**.<br>• Release a **community‑driven “Optimization Recipes”** library (e.g., tree‑shaking for React, dead‑code elimination for Vue). | **Adoption rate** – % of trial users who add `js-optimizer` to `package.json` in production (goal ≥ 60 %). |
| **Expand** | • Team notices faster page‑load times and lower CDN costs; product manager asks “What else can we optimize?”. | • Lack of visibility beyond JavaScript (e.g., CSS, assets).<br>• Need for deeper performance insights (runtime profiling). | Excited, looking for more value. | • Bundle **js‑optimizer Pro**: adds CSS minification, WASM‑based image compression, and a **performance analytics plugin** that reports bundle size trends over time.<br>• Offer **enterprise support SLA** and a **custom rule engine** for company‑specific optimizations. | **Expansion revenue** – % of adopters upgrading to Pro or purchasing support (goal ≥ 25 %). |

### Narrative Flow (quick read)

1. **Aware** – Devs discover the pain of slow builds → we capture attention with clear, data‑driven benchmarks.
2. **Consider** – They evaluate effort → we lower integration friction with auto‑config and clear docs.
3. **Try** – Hands‑on test with safety nets → real‑time feedback and automatic rollback keep confidence high.
4. **Adopt** – Proven ROI → seamless upgrades and community recipes keep the tool in the stack.
5. **Expand** – New performance goals → Pro features and enterprise support turn a single‑tool adoption into a broader optimization platform.
